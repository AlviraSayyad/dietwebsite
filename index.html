Keto Meal Plan

The keto diet is all about eating meals that are high in fat, consist moderate portions of protein and very low in carbs. The reduction of carbohydrates results in a metabolic process called ketosis where your body fat is then used to generate energy in the body.

Mediterranean Diet

The Mediterranean Diet is characterized by a diverse and balanced approach to eating, emphasizing whole, minimally processed foods. It draws inspiration from the dietary habits of countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea, such as Greece, Italy, and Spain.

Vegetarian Diet plan

The Vegetarian Diet is a plant-based eating plan that excludes meat and, in some cases, other animal products. It focuses on consuming a variety of fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds to meet nutritional needs. Here's a brief description of the Vegetarian Diet plan: Description: The Vegetarian Diet is a lifestyle choice that prioritizes plant-based foods while abstaining from meat. It is characterized by a rich and diverse array of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds. This diet not only eliminates the consumption of red meat, poultry, and seafood but also promotes ethical and environmental considerations by reducing reliance on animal products.

Intermittent Fasting

Involves cycles of eating and fasting, with various approaches like 16/8 (16 hours fasting, 8 hours eating) or 5:2 (eating normally for five days and restricting calories for two days).

Atkins Diet

The Atkins Diet is a low-carbohydrate diet designed for weight loss and improved overall health. It was developed by Dr. Robert C. Atkins in the 1960s and has undergone several revisions over the years. The fundamental principle of the Atkins Diet is to restrict carbohydrate intake to induce a state of ketosis, where the body primarily burns fat for energy.
